## Slide 6: Manufacturing Processes (~50 sec)
**Speaker**: Member B

- Focus on the 3 multi-process parts:

  3. PCB: etching → drilling → SMT → reflow solder
- Highlight that current design uses permanent joining (ultrasonic welding / adhesives)

**Visual**: Process flow diagram for each multi-process part. Simple icons or photos.

## Slide 7: DfD Principles Applied (~60 sec)
**Speaker**: Member C

- Top 5 DfD principles most relevant:
  1. Reversible fasteners (snap-fits replace welding)
  2. Modular architecture (3 separable modules)
  3. Hazardous components most accessible (battery first out)
  4. Material marking (ISO codes, QR for recycling)
  5. Minimize disassembly steps (1 step for battery, <5 total)
- For each: how current design VIOLATES the principle

**Visual**: Two-column layout — "Current" vs. "DfD Redesign" for each principle. Icons or simple diagrams.

## General Presentation Tips

- **Font size**: Minimum 24pt for body text, 36pt+ for titles
- **Photos**: Use your OWN disassembly photos — they demonstrate hands-on work
- **Timing**: Practice to fit in 10 minutes. Each slide ~50 seconds.
- **Transitions**: Smooth handoffs between speakers. Each person covers 3 slides.
- **Member distribution**:
  - Member A: Slides 2-3 (problem + product)
  - Member B: Slides 4-6 (disassembly + materials + processes)
  - Member C: Slides 7-8 (DfD principles + reference LCA)
  - Member D: Slides 9-11 (standard + comparison LCA + results)
  - All: Slides 1, 12 (title + conclusions)
