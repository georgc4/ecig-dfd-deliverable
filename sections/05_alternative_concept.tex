\section{Alternative Concept: The Universal Removable Battery Standard}
\label{sec:alternative}

The LCA results (Section~\ref{sec:lca}) identified \textbf{primary aluminum manufacturing} as the single largest contributor to the environmental impact of the Air Bar NEX. The DfD concept addresses this by switching the housing to recycled (secondary) aluminum, which alone produced the greatest reduction in overall impact. However, the DfD analysis also revealed that the lithium-ion battery---while not the dominant LCA driver---remains the most problematic component at end of life: it is classified as hazardous waste, causes fires in waste facilities costing \$95M/year \cite{wmw2025}, and its constituent metals (cobalt, nickel, lithium) are critical minerals with constrained supply chains. This section presents an alternative concept that builds on the DfD redesign by introducing a \textbf{universal removable battery standard} for electronic cigarettes, targeting both environmental impact reduction and end-of-life safety.

\subsection{Design Rationale}

The Oxford/UCL study demonstrated that lithium-ion cells recovered from disposable e-cigarettes can sustain over 450 charge--discharge cycles \cite{williams2023}, yet each cell is discarded after a single use. Decoupling the battery from the consumable portion of the device and standardizing it across manufacturers enables:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Battery lifecycle extension}: Reuse across 10--450+ device lifecycles rather than 1, reducing per-device demand for cobalt, nickel, and lithium
    \item \textbf{Fire prevention}: Batteries removed before the device enters the waste stream, eliminating the ignition source in waste facilities
    \item \textbf{Regulatory compliance}: The EU Batteries Regulation (Article~11) mandates removable, replaceable batteries in portable electronics by February 2027 \cite{eu_batteries_2023}
    \item \textbf{Consumer participation}: A simple, tool-free removal mechanism lowers the barrier to proper disposal and recycling
\end{itemize}

This approach draws on precedent from the AA/AAA battery standard (IEC 60086), which enables interchangeability across thousands of products, and the Fairphone modular smartphone, which demonstrates commercial viability of DfD consumer electronics.

\subsection{Proposed Standard Specification}

The standard defines \textbf{three battery form factors} covering the full range of disposable e-cigarette capacities:

The VC-M size class corresponds to the recovered 13450-format cell from the Air Bar NEX (12.0\,mm diameter, 40.0\,mm length, 650\,mAh, 3.7\,V). The prototype CAD model (Section~\ref{sec:cad_prototype}) is designed around this cell.

\subsubsection{Mechanical Interface}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Battery compartment}: Located at the base of the device, accessible via a quarter-turn bayonet (twist-lock) base cap
    \item \textbf{Insertion method}: Drop-in cylindrical cell with keyed orientation (polarity alignment via asymmetric notch in the battery sled)
    \item \textbf{Retention}: Spring-loaded contact at one end provides both electrical connection and mechanical retention
    \item \textbf{Removal}: Single-handed operation --- quarter-turn the base cap, tilt the device, and the battery slides out
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Electrical Interface}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Contacts}: Gold-plated spring contacts at positive and negative terminals
    \item \textbf{Nominal voltage}: 3.7\,V (standard Li-ion)
    \item \textbf{Protection}: Each battery cell includes an integrated protection circuit (overcharge, over-discharge, short-circuit)
    \item \textbf{Charging}: Standardized USB-C charging port on the device body (not on the battery)
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Identification and Labeling}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Color coding}: Each size class has a distinct color band (e.g., Blue = S, Green = M, Orange = L)
    \item \textbf{QR code}: Links to recycling instructions, battery chemistry, manufacturer, and date of production (per EU Batteries Regulation requirements)
    \item \textbf{Material marking}: Battery sleeve marked with Li-ion recycling symbol and chemistry code
\end{itemize}

Key design changes from the current Air Bar NEX:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Housing material}: Switch from primary aluminum to \textbf{recycled (secondary) aluminum} or \textbf{mono-material recyclable plastic} (polypropylene). The LCA showed that this single change produces the greatest reduction in environmental impact.
    \item \textbf{Push-button snap-fit assembly}: The mouthpiece insert features two push-button snap-fit tabs (10.0\,mm wide $\times$ 4.0\,mm tall) that protrude through matching cutouts in the aluminum shell, locking the mouthpiece in place. Living hinge cutouts (vertical side slits + horizontal relief) in the mouthpiece body allow the tabs to flex inward when pressed, enabling tool-free removal --- no adhesives, no ultrasonic welding, no screws.
    \item \textbf{Battery compartment}: Twist-lock (bayonet) base cap with quarter-turn engagement. Battery sits in a carrier sled with a polarity key notch to prevent reverse insertion.
    \item \textbf{Modular atomizer cartridge}: Slide-in cartridge (16.0\,mm OD, 35.0\,mm length) containing the coil, wick, and e-liquid reservoir. Retained by an O-ring groove and cartridge lip. Enables separate recycling from the battery and housing.
    \item \textbf{USB-C charging port}: Integrated into the body shell, allowing the battery to be recharged in-device without removal.
    \item \textbf{PCB mounting}: Press-fit standoffs on an internal bracket; PCB removable without tools or soldering.
\end{enumerate}

The overall device dimensions (48.2\,mm width $\times$ 19.0\,mm depth $\times$ 78.3\,mm height, with 0.7\,mm shell wall thickness) are faithful to the measured Air Bar NEX form factor while accommodating the modular snap-fit architecture.

\subsection{Impact Reduction Mechanisms}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Aluminum impact reduction}: Switching from primary to recycled aluminum for the housing eliminates the dominant environmental impact identified in the LCA. Secondary aluminum production requires approximately 95\% less energy than primary smelting.
    \item \textbf{Battery lifecycle extension}: Each battery reused across 10--450+ device lifecycles instead of 1, reducing per-use demand for lithium, cobalt, and nickel
    \item \textbf{Elimination of waste stream fires}: Batteries removed before disposal, preventing lithium-ion fires in waste facilities
    \item \textbf{Material recovery}: $>$90\% battery material recovery through established Li-ion recycling pathways (vs.\ $\sim$0\% currently)
    \item \textbf{Simplified recycling}: Mono-material housing enables single-stream recycling; modular separation eliminates manual sorting at recycling facilities
\end{enumerate}

The DfD concept LCA in Section~\ref{sec:lca} already models the primary environmental benefits of this alternative concept, including the switch to recycled aluminum and the change from landfill to recycling at end of life. A dedicated LCA for the battery-reuse scenario was not performed; however, allocating battery manufacturing impacts across even 10 reuse cycles would reduce per-device battery contribution by approximately 90\%.
